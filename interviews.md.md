# Week 1: Trader Interviews – AfriTrade Agent

**Crew:** Team‑Africod  
**Interviewers:** 1. Zerihun Asrat (Ethiopia),  2. Aja Corneli (Nigeria) 3. Patience Nzomuhoza (Burundi) 
**Dates:** 20–23 May 2026  
**Method:** WhatsApp and Telegram voice calls, recorded with consent (anonymised)

## Ethiopia – Zerihun Asrat (2 interviews, 1 incomplet)

### T‑ET01 – Habtamu Tsegaye (Electronics trader, Addis Ababa to Nairobi route)
- **Product:** Green coffee beans (5–10 sacks per trip)
- **Border crossing:** Moyale (Ethiopia–Kenya)
- **Frequency:** Twice a month
- **Current pain:** “The transit fee changes every week. Nobody publishes it. I have to ask three different brokers and take the average.”
- **Time wasted:** 3–4 hours per crossing
- **Unofficial payment:** ~300 birr ($5.50) for “quick clearance”
- **Quote:** “If my phone could tell me the real fee at Moyale, I would not need to leave at 3am.”
- **Language preference:** Amharic or simple English, Tigringa and Oromo

### T‑ET02 – Woynishet Tekle (Honey, Khat and butter trader, Addis to Djibouti)
- **Product:** Fresh khat leaves, honey (as a market need)
- **Border crossing:** (Ethiopia–Djibouti)
- **Frequency:** Weekly or twice a month
- **Current pain:** “I need to know if the port is backed up. One hour delay = my khat is worthless.”
- **Time wasted:** unknown (may be 3 hours on average mainly checking with friends)
- **Language preference:** Amharic, Oromo

## Nigeria – Aja Cornel (proposed 3 interviews)
### T‑NG01 – Fatima (phone parts, Lagos to Accra)
- **Product:** 50–100 smartphone screens, batteries
- **Border crossing:** Seme (Nigeria–Benin–Ghana corridor)
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Current pain:** “I pay a broker 10% of my goods just to tell me the tariff. That is 50,000 naira for nothing.”
- **Time wasted:** 4–5 hours
- **Current solution:** Broker + guesswork
- **Quote:** “The ECOWAS PDF is too long. I cannot read it. Just tell me: 12% or 20%?”
- **Language preference:** Pidgin English

### T‑NG02 – Amara (rice trader, Lagos to Cotonou, Benin)
- **Product:** Parboiled rice (50kg bags)
- **Border crossing:** Seme (same as Fatima)
- **Frequency:** Twice a week
- **Current pain:** “There is a 5% levy on rice that comes and goes. Nobody announces it. I overpay 20% sometimes.”
- **Time wasted:** 2–3 hours asking at the border
- **Quote:** “Give me a number I can trust. I will even send a voice message if I see a new levy.”
- **Language preference:** English / Pidgin

## Burundi – Patience Nzomuhoza (2 interviews) 

### T‑BI01 – Jean (beans trader, Bujumbura to Goma, DRC)
- **Product:** Dried beans (sacks)
- **Border crossing:** Uvira (Burundi–DRC)
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Current pain:** “I don’t know which route is safe. Armed groups sometimes block the road. I lose my beans.”
- **Time wasted:** 2–3 hours asking drivers
- **Quote:** “Safety first. I need a voice that tells me: ‘route clear’ or ‘avoid today’.”
- **Language preference:** Kirundi / French

### T‑BI02 – Claudine (palm oil, Bujumbura to Kigali, Rwanda)
- **Product:** Palm oil in 20L jerricans
- **Border crossing:** Kobero (Burundi–Rwanda)
- **Frequency:** Bi‑weekly
- **Current pain:** “The customs officer asks for documents I don’t have. I pay $5 for someone to ‘explain’.”
- **Time wasted:** 3 hours
- **Quote:** “What papers do I need? Just tell me. I can prepare before I leave.”
- **Language preference:** Kirundi / Swahili
